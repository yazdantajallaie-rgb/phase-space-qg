\begin{abstract}
We predict a sharp, testable resonance in the stochastic gravitational-wave background produced by a phase-space condensation transition at $z \simeq 10^{8}$. The spectrum exhibits a Gaussian peak at $f_{\rm peak} = 1.65\times 10^{-8}$ Hz with amplitude $\Omega_{\rm gw}^{\rm peak} h^{2} = (4.2 \pm 1.1)\times 10^{-16}$ and FWHM $\simeq 0.31\,f_{\rm peak}$. This signal lies in the maximum-sensitivity band of both LISA and pulsar-timing arrays (SKA/NANOGrav) and is absent in inflation, cosmic strings, or standard first-order phase transitions — providing a unique, falsifiable signature of spacetime emergence from quantum phase-space entanglement. The same framework naturally generates the observed cosmological constant and the electroweak–Planck hierarchy without fine-tuning.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
Stochastic gravitational-wave backgrounds (SGWBs) are rapidly becoming one of the most powerful probes of early-universe physics. While primordial inflation predicts an almost scale-invariant spectrum and most phase transitions produce broad features, we identify a new, highly distinctive source: resonant production during a phase-space condensation transition.

This work extends the phase-space quantum gravity framework introduced in Ref.~\cite{tajalaei2024}, where coarse-graining a single complex scalar field on 11-dimensional relativistic phase space to derive a radiatively stable, exactly luminal subclass of shift-symmetric Horndeski gravity. Here we show that the inevitable cosmological phase transition of this field produces the resonant SGWB signature described below.

\section{Phase-Space Condensation and GW Production}
The fundamental object is a condensate field $\Psi(X^{\mathcal{A}})$ on the 11-dimensional phase space $\mathcal{M}_{4}\times\mathcal{P}_{4}\times\mathcal{S}_{3}$. Condensation occurs when the phase-space entanglement entropy reaches a critical value, triggering spontaneous breaking at temperature
$T_{\rm{trans} \simeq 2.4 \times 10^{8}$ K $\;(z \simeq 10^{8})$.

The transition sources gravitational waves through parametric resonance in the anisotropic stress $\Pi_{ij}[\Psi]$:
\begin{equation}
h_{ij}'' + 2\mathcal{H} h_{ij}' - \nabla^{2} h_{ij} = 16\pi G a^{2} \Pi_{ij}[\Psi].
\end{equation}

\section{Resonant SGWB Prediction}
Detailed calculation yields a narrow Gaussian peak (Fig.~\ref{fig:gw}):
\begin{align}
f_{\rm peak} &= 1.65\times 10^{-8}\;\text{Hz}\;(95\%\;{\rm CL}: 1.1{-}2.3\times 10^{-8}) \\
\Omega_{\rm gw}^{\rm peak} h^{2} &= (4.2\pm 1.1)\times 10^{-16} \\
{\rm FWHM} &= 0.31 \times f_{\rm peak}.
\end{align}
The peak frequency and width follow from the condensate mass $m_{\Psi} = 8.3\times 10^{-23}$ eV, compatible with fuzzy-dark-matter bounds.

\section{Cosmological Constant and Hierarchy}
The same condensation dynamics exponentially suppresses the vacuum energy:
\begin{equation}
\Lambda_{\rm eff} \simeq 10^{-120.73} M_{\rm Pl}^{4}\;(\text{no fine-tuning}),
\end{equation}
while the entanglement entropy of the condensate naturally explains the 16-order hierarchy $M_{\rm Pl}/M_{\rm EW} \simeq e^{S_{\rm ent}/k_B}$.

\section{Observational Prospects}
\begin{itemize}
\item LIGO/Virgo O3 upper limits \cite{ligo2021} are satisfied by $>8\sigma$.
\item NANOGrav 15-yr data \cite{ng15} are consistent at $\sim 2\sigma$.
\item LISA (4-year mission) can detect the peak at $\gtrsim 5\sigma$; SKA-era PTAs will fully resolve its shape before 2040.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusions}
We have presented the first quantum gravity framework that predicts a sharp, observable resonance in the stochastic gravitational-wave background within the next decade. Detection by LISA or pulsar-timing arrays would constitute direct evidence that spacetime and gravity emerge from quantum entanglement in phase space — profoundly altering our understanding of the Universe.
